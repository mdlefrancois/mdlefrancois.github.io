Summary: Latitude versus X


This project visualizes the weather of 500+ cities across the word far from and closer to the equator at 0Â° Latitude. Latitude is a measurement of distance in degrees north or south of the Equator. Ninety degrees of parallel latitude lines reside from the Equator to each of the poles (north or south). The Equator divides the Earth in half and it's the largest parallel on Earth. Latitude lines above the Equator are "north" in the northern hemisphere. Latitude lines below the Equator are "south" in the southern hemisphere (negative degrees of latitude).

To understand how atmospheric data changes away from and near the Equator country information containing: temperature, cloudiness, humidity, and wind speed were pulled from the Open Weather Map API and presented graphicaly from September 2018. This site combines source data and visualizations created as part of the analysis.
